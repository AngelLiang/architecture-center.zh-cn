# <a name="establishing-an-operational-fitness-review"></a><span data-ttu-id="8ba78-103">建立操作适用性评审</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-103">Establishing an operational fitness review</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-104">企业开始在 Azure 中操作工作负荷以后，下一步是建立**操作适用性评审**过程，以便枚举、实现并以迭代方式评审这些工作负荷的**非功能性**要求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-104">As your enterprise begins to operate workloads in Azure, the next step is to establish an **operational fitness review** process to enumerate, implement, and iteratively review the **non-functional** requirements for these workloads.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-105">非功能性要求与服务的预期操作行为相关。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-105">_Non-functional_ requirements are related to the expected operational behavior of the service.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-106">有五种基本类别的非功能性要求，称为[软件质量的要素](../../guide/pillars.md)：可伸缩性、可用性、复原能力（包括业务连续性和灾难恢复）、管理和安全性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-106">There are five essential categories of non-functional requirements referred to as the [pillars of software quality](../../guide/pillars.md): scalability, availability, resiliency (including business continuity and disaster recovery), management, and security.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-107">操作适用性评审过程的目的是确保任务关键型工作负荷满足企业对质量要素的预期。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-107">The purpose of an operational fitness review process is ensuring that your mission critical workloads meet the expectations of your business with respect to the quality pillars.</span></span>

## <a name="understanding-the-problem"></a><span data-ttu-id="8ba78-114">了解问题</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-114">Understanding the problem</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-115">根据[入门](../../cloud-adoption/getting-started/overview.md)中的介绍，在企业数字化转型过程中，第一步是确定需要通过 Azure 来解决的业务问题。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-115">As you learned in [Getting started](../../cloud-adoption/getting-started/overview.md), the first step in an enterprise's digital transformation is identifying the business problems to be solved by adopting Azure.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-116">下一步是确定问题的高级解决方案，例如将工作负荷迁移到云，或者调整现有的本地服务，使之包含云功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-116">The next step is to determine a high-level solution to the problem, such as migrating a workload to the cloud, or adapting an existing on-premises service to include cloud functionality.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-117">最后，设计并实施解决方案。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-117">Finally, the solution is designed and implemented.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-118">在此过程中，通常关注服务的功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-118">During this process, the focus is often on the _features_ of the service.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-119">也就是说，有一系列功能性要求需要服务来执行。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-119">That is, there are a set of desired _functional_ requirements for the service to perform.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-120">例如，产品交付服务需要相关功能来确定产品的源和目标位置，在完成交付、客户通知等操作时对产品进行跟踪。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-120">For example, a product delivery service requires features for determining the source and destination locations of the product, tracking the product during delivery, customer notifications, and others.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-121">与此相反，非功能性要求则与服务的[可用性](../../checklist/availability.md)、[复原能力](../../resiliency/index.md)和[可伸缩性](../../checklist/scalability.md)等属性相关。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-121">In contrast, the _non-functional_ requirements relate to properties such as the service's [availability](../../checklist/availability.md), [resiliency](../../resiliency/index.md), and [scalability](../../checklist/scalability.md).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-122">这些属性不同于功能性要求，因为它们不直接影响服务中任何具体特性的最终功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-122">These properties differ from the functional requirements because they do not directly affect the final function of any particular feature in the service.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-123">但是，这些非功能性要求与服务的性能和连续性相关。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-123">However, these non-functional requirements are related to the _performance_ and _continuity_ of the service.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-124">某些非功能性要求可以通过服务级别协议 (SLA) 条款来指定。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-124">Some non-functional requirements can be specified in terms of a service level agreement (SLA).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-125">例如，就服务连续性来说，可以将服务的可用性要求表述为某个百分比，比如**在 99.99% 的时间可用**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-125">For example, with regard to service continuity, an availability requirement for the service can be expressed as a percentage such as **available 99.99% of the time**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-126">其他非功能性要求可能更加难以定义，并且可能随着生产需求的变化而变化。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-126">Other non-functional requirements may be more difficult to define and may change as production needs evolve.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-127">例如，某个面向使用者的服务在热门过后可能会开始面临非预期的吞吐量要求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-127">For example, a consumer-facing service might start facing unanticipated throughput requirements after a surge of popularity.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-128">![NOTE] 若要更深入地了解如何定义复原能力要求，包括如何对 RPO、RTO、SLA 和相关概念进行说明，请参阅[设计适用于 Azure 的可复原应用程序](../../resiliency/index.md#define-your-availability-requirements)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-128">![NOTE] Defining the requirements for resiliency, including explanations of RPO, RTO, SLA, and related concepts, are explored in more depth in [Designing resilient applications for Azure](../../resiliency/index.md#define-your-availability-requirements).</span></span>

## <a name="operational-fitness-review-process"></a><span data-ttu-id="8ba78-129">操作适用性评审过程</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-129">Operational fitness review process</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-130">若要维护企业服务的性能和连续性，关键是实施操作适用性评审过程。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-130">The key to maintaining the performance and continuity of an enterprise's services is to implement an _operational fitness review_ process.</span></span>

![操作适用性评审过程概述](_images/ofr-flow.png)

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-132">此过程大致分为两个阶段。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-132">At a high level, the process has two phases.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-133">在先决条件阶段，需确定要求并将其映射到支持服务。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-133">In the prerequisites phase, the requirements are established and mapped to supporting services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-134">此情况出现频率较低；也许每年出现一次，或者在引入新操作时出现。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-134">This occurs less frequently; perhaps annually or when new operations are introduced.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-135">先决条件阶段的输出在流阶段使用。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-135">The output of the prerequisites phase is used in the flow phase.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-136">流阶段出现频率较高；建议每月一次。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-136">The flow phase occurs more frequently; we recommend monthly.</span></span>

### <a name="prerequisites-phase"></a><span data-ttu-id="8ba78-137">先决条件阶段</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-137">Prerequisites phase</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-138">此阶段的步骤用于捕获对重要服务进行定期评审所需满足的要求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-138">The steps in this phase are intended to capture the necessary requirements for conducting a regular review of the important services.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-139">**确定关键业务操作**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-139">**Identify critical business operations**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-140">确定企业的**任务关键型**业务操作。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-140">Identify the enterprise's **mission critical** business operations.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-141">业务操作独立于任何支持服务功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-141">Business operations are independent from any supporting service functionality.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-142">换句话说，业务操作代表企业需执行的实际活动，受一组 IT 服务的支持。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-142">In other words, business operations represent the actual activities that the business needs to perform and are supported by a set of IT services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-143">术语“任务关键型”或“业务关键型”反映了在操作受阻的情况下对业务的严重影响。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-143">The term _mission critical_, or alternatively _business critical_, reflects a severe impact to the business if the operation is impeded.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-144">例如，在线零售商可能会有“允许客户向购物车添加商品”或“处理信用卡付款”之类的业务操作。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-144">For example, an online retailer may have a business operation such as "enable a customer to add an item to a shopping cart" or "process a credit card payment".</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-145">如果其中一项操作失败，客户将无法完成交易，企业将无法完成销售。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-145">If either of these operations were to fail, a customer would be unable to complete the transaction and the enterprise would fail to realize sales.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-146">**将操作映射到服务**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-146">**Map operations to services**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-147">将这些业务操作映射到支持它们的服务。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-147">Map these business operations to the services that support them.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-148">在上面的购物车示例中，可能涉及多项服务：库存管理服务、购物车服务，等等。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-148">In the above shopping cart example, several services may be involved: an inventory stock management service, a shopping cart service, and others.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-149">在上面的信用卡付款示例中，本地付款服务可能会与第三方款项处理服务交互。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-149">In the credit card payment example above, an on-premises payment service may interact with a third-party payment processing service.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-150">**分析服务依赖关系**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-150">**Analyze service dependencies**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-151">大多数业务操作需要在多项支持服务之间进行协调。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-151">Most business operations require orchestration between multiple supporting services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-152">必须了解服务之间的依赖关系以及任务关键型事务在这些服务之间的流动。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-152">It is important to understand the dependences between the services and the flow of mission critical transactions through these services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-153">另外还应考虑本地服务和 Azure 服务之间的依赖关系。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-153">You should also consider the dependencies between on-premises services and Azure services.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-154">在购物车示例中，库存管理服务可能托管在本地，通过员工从某个物理仓库引入数据输入，但它可能将数据存储在 Azure 服务（例如 [Azure 存储](/azure/storage/common/storage-introduction)）中或数据库（例如 [Azure Cosmos DB](/azure/cosmos-db/introduction)）中。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-154">In the shopping cart example, the inventory stock management service may be hosted on-premises and ingest data input by employees from a physical warehouse, but it may store data in an Azure service such as [Azure storage](/azure/storage/common/storage-introduction) or a database such as [Azure Cosmos DB](/azure/cosmos-db/introduction).</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-155">这些活动的输出是一组适用于服务操作的**记分卡指标**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-155">An output from these activities is a set of **scorecard metrics** for service operations.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-156">这些指标按照非功能性条件（例如可用性、可伸缩性和灾难恢复）进行分类。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-156">The metrics are categorized in terms of non-functional criteria such as availability, scalability, and disaster recovery.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-157">记分卡指标表述服务在操作方面应满足的条件。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-157">Scorecard metrics express the criteria that the service is expected to meet operationally.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-158">这些指标可以在任何粒度级别表述，只要该级别适合服务操作。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-158">These metrics can be expressed at any level of granularity that is appropriate for the service operation.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-159">记分卡应该以简单的术语表述，方便业务负责人和工程人员进行有意义的讨论。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-159">The scorecard should be expressed in simple terms to facilitate meaningful discussion between the business owners and engineering.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-160">例如，可伸缩性记分卡指标为绿色时，表示性能符合所需标准；为黄色时，表示性能不符合所需标准，但正在积极实施计划的补救措施；为红色时，表示性能不符合所需标准，且没有任何计划或措施。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-160">For example, a scalability scorecard metric could be expressed as _green_ for performing at the desired criteria, _yellow_ for failing to meet the desired criteria but actively implementing a planned remediation, and _red_ for failing to meet the desired criteria with no plan or action.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-161">必须强调的是，这些指标应该直接反映业务需求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-161">It is important to emphasize that these metrics should directly reflect business needs.</span></span>

### <a name="service-review-phase"></a><span data-ttu-id="8ba78-162">服务评审阶段</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-162">Service review phase</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-163">服务评审阶段是操作适用性评审过程的核心。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-163">The service review phase is core of the operational fitness review process.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-164">**衡量服务指标**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-164">**Measure service metrics**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-165">使用记分卡指标时，应对服务进行监视，确保其符合业务预期。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-165">Using the scorecard metrics, the services should be monitored to ensure that they meet the business expectations.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-166">这意味着服务监视很重要。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-166">This means that service monitoring is essential.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-167">如果无法针对非功能性要求监视一组服务，则应将相应的记分卡指标视为红色。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-167">If you are not able to monitor a set of services with respect to the non-functional requirements, then the corresponding scorecard metrics should be considered red.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-168">在这种情况下，若要进行补救，第一步是实施适当的服务监视。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-168">In this case, the first step for remediation is to implement the appropriate service monitoring.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="8ba78-169">例如，如果企业预期某项服务在操作时的可用性为 99.99%，但尚未准备好生产遥测来衡量可用性，则应假定你不符合要求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-169">For example, if the business expects a service to operate with 99.99% availability, but there is no production telemetry in place to measure the availability, you should assume that you're not meeting the requirement.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-170">**计划补救**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-170">**Plan remediation**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-171">对于其指标低于某个可接受阈值的每项服务操作，请确定对服务进行补救以使操作指标回到可接受范围的成本。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-171">For each service operation with metrics that fall below an acceptable threshold, determine the cost of remediating the service to bring operation to an acceptable metric.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-172">如果补救该服务的成本高于该服务预期会产生的收入，则应考虑无形成本，例如客户体验成本。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-172">If the cost of remediating the service is greater than the expected revenue generation of the service, move on to consider the non-tangible costs such as customer experience.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-173">例如，客户在难以使用该服务成功下单时，可能会改为选择你的竞争对手下单。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-173">For example, if customers have difficulty placing a successful order using the service, they may choose a competitor instead.</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-174">**实施补救**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-174">**Implement remediation**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-175">在业务负责人和工程人员共同制定某项计划以后，就应该实施该计划。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-175">After the business owners and engineering converge on a plan, it should be implemented.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="8ba78-176">只要评审记分卡指标，就应该报告实施状态。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="8ba78-176">The status of the implementation should be reported whenever scorecard metrics are reviewed.</span></span>
