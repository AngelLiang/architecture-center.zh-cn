<span data-ttu-id="50ceb-109">**谬论：云始终更便宜。**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-109">**Myth: Cloud is always cheaper.**</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-110">一种共识是，在云中运营数据中心始终比在本地运营更便宜。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-110">It's a common belief that operating a datacenter in the cloud is always cheaper than on-premises.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-111">尽管这种说法有道理，但并不绝对正确。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-111">While this can be true, it's not an absolute.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-112">在某些情况下，云运营成本实际上可能更高。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-112">In some cases, cloud operating costs can actually be higher.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-113">造成这种局面的常见原因包括成本控制不佳、系统体系结构不符、过程重复、特殊的系统配置，或人工成本增加。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-113">Often this is caused by poor cost governance, misalignment of system architectures, duplication of processes, unusual system configurations, or increased staffing costs.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-114">幸运的是，可以缓解其中的许多问题来产生前期 ROI。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-114">Luckily, many of these problems can be mitigated to create early ROI.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-115">可以遵循[构建业务理由](#building-the-business-justification)中的指导来帮助检测和避免这些不合规的做法。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-115">Following the guidance in [Building the business justification](#building-the-business-justification) can help detect and avoid these misalignments.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-116">还可以参考本文揭示的其他谬论。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-116">Dispelling the other myths listed here can also help.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="50ceb-117">**谬论：应将所有内容迁移到云中。**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-117">**Myth: Everything should go into the cloud.**</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-118">事实上，有许多业务推动因素可能导致你选择混合解决方案。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-118">In fact, there are a number of business drivers that may lead you to choose a hybrid solution.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-119">在最终确定业务模型之前，明智的做法是根据[数字资产](../digital-estate/5-rs-of-rationalization.md)文章中所述完成第一轮量化分析。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-119">Before finalizing the business model, it's wise to complete a first round quantitative analysis as described in the [Digital Estate articles](../digital-estate/5-rs-of-rationalization.md).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-120">有关合理化涉及的各种量化推动因素的其他信息，请参阅[合理化的 5 个 R](../digital-estate/5-rs-of-rationalization.md)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-120">For additional information on the individual quantitative drivers involved in rationalization, see [The 5 Rs of rationalization](../digital-estate/5-rs-of-rationalization.md).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-121">每种方法将使用可轻松获取的库存数据以及简要的量化分析，来识别可能在云中造成更高成本的工作负荷或应用程序。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-121">Either approach will use easily obtained inventory data and a brief quantitative analysis to identify workloads or applications that could result in higher costs in the cloud.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-122">这些方法还可以识别混合解决方案必需的依赖项或流量模式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-122">These approaches could also identify dependencies or traffic patterns that would necessitate a hybrid solution.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="50ceb-123">**谬论：镜像本地环境有助于在云中节省资金。**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-123">**Myth: Mirroring my on-premises environment will help me save money in the cloud.**</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-124">我们时常听到，在数字资产规划期间，客户检测到未使用的容量超过了预配环境的 50%。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-124">During Digital Estate planning, it's not unheard of for customers to detect unused capacity in excess of 50% of the provisioned environment.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-125">如果在云中预配的资产与当前预配量相当，则很难实现成本节省。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-125">If assets are provisioned in the cloud to match current provisioning, cost savings will be hard to realize.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-126">考虑缩小部署资产的规模，以便与使用模式相符，而不是与预配模式相符。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-126">Consider reducing the size of the deployed assets to align with usage patterns, not provisioning patterns.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="50ceb-127">**谬论：服务器成本推动了云迁移的业务案例。**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-127">**Myth: Server costs drive the business cases for cloud migration.**</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-128">这种说法有时是正确的。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-128">Sometimes this is true.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-129">对于某些公司而言，减少与服务器相关的持续资本性支出非常重要。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-129">For some companies, it's important to reduce ongoing capital expenses related to servers.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-130">但是，这取决于几个因素。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-130">However, this depends on several factors.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-131">硬件更新周期为五到八年的公司很难从云迁移中快速获得回报。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-131">Companies with a five- to eight-year hardware refresh cycle are unlikely to see fast returns on their cloud migration.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-132">采用标准化或强制更新周期的公司可能很快会实现收支平衡。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-132">Companies with standardized or enforced refresh cycles can hit a break-even point quickly.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-133">在任一情况下，其他支出可能是充当迁移理由的财务触发器。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-133">In either case, other expenses may be the financial triggers that justify the migration.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-134">以下是几个示例是创建仅包括服务器或仅包括 VM 的成本视图时往往被忽视的成本：</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-134">The following are a few examples of costs that are commonly overlooked when taking a server-only or VM-only view of costs:</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-135">虚拟化、服务器和中间件的软件成本可能很高。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-135">Software costs of virtualization, servers, and middleware can be extensive.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-136">云提供商消除了其中的一些成本。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-136">Cloud providers eliminate some of these costs.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-137">[Azure 混合权益](https://azure.microsoft.com/pricing/hybrid-benefit/#services)和[预订](https://azure.microsoft.com/reservations/)计划就是云提供商降低虚拟化成本的两个示例。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-137">Two examples of a cloud provider reducing virtualization costs are the [Azure Hybrid Benefits](https://azure.microsoft.com/pricing/hybrid-benefit/#services) and [Reservations](https://azure.microsoft.com/reservations/) programs.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-138">服务中断造成的业务损失可能很快就会超过硬件或软件成本。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-138">Business losses due to outages can quickly exceed hardware or software costs.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-139">如果当前数据中心不稳定，可与业务部门协作，以量化服务中断对机会成本或实际业务成本造成的影响。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-139">If the current datacenter is unstable, work with the business to quantify the impact of outages in terms of opportunity costs or actual business costs.</span></span>
- <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-140">环境成本也可能造成影响。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-140">Environmental costs can also be impactful.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-141">对于普通的美国家庭而言，他们的家庭开支在预算中是最大的投资和成本。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-141">For the average American family, their home is the biggest investment and highest cost in their budget.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-142">数据中心也是如此。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-142">The same is often true for data centers.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-143">不动产、设备和公用设施成本代表了本地成本的相当大一部分。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-143">Real estate, facilities, and utility costs represent a fair portion of on-premises costs.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-144">数据中心停用后，业务部门可以重新安排这些设备的用途，或者其整个业务成本可能被消除。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-144">When data centers are retired, those facilities can be repurposed by the business, or potentially the business could be released from the costs entirely.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="50ceb-145">**谬论：运营支出 (OpEx) 优于资本支出 (CapEx)。**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-145">**Myth: Operating Expense (OpEx) is better than Capital Expense (CapEx).**</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-146">如[财务成果](business-outcomes/fiscal-outcomes.md)一文中所述，OpEx 可能是一件好事。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-146">As explained in the [fiscal outcomes](business-outcomes/fiscal-outcomes.md) article, OpEx can be a good thing.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-147">但是，许多行业可能将 OpEx 视为一种负面因素。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-147">However, there are a number of industries that can see OpEx as a negative.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-148">以下几个示例会在 OpEx 会话中，触发与会计和业务单位的更紧密集成：</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-148">The following are a few examples that would trigger tighter integration with the accounting and business units regarding the OpEx conversation:</span></span>

- <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-151">在私人股权公司拥有的企业或寻求资本输入的企业中，增加 OpEx 也可能被认为会产生不利的结果。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-151">OpEx increases can also be seen as a negative outcome in businesses owned by a private equity firm or seeking capital influx.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="50ceb-153">OpEx 并不总是一件坏事。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-153">OpEx is not always a bad thing.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-154">企业更倾向于将 OpEx 视为比 CapEx 更为有利。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-154">Businesses are more likely to see OpEx as more favorable than CapEx.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-155">例如，试图改善现金流、降低资本投资或减少资产持有量的企业可能很乐意接受这种方法。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-155">For instance, this approach can be well received by businesses that are attempting to improve cash flow, reduce capital investments, or decrease asset holdings.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="50ceb-156">在提供注重于从 CapEx 到 OpEx 的转换的业务理由之前，请了解哪种方法对企业更有利。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-156">Before providing a business justification that focuses on a conversion from CapEx to OpEx, understand which is better for the business.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-157">会计和采购部门通常可以帮助根据财务目标以最佳方式来与传达的信息保持一致。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-157">Accounting and procurement can often help best align the message to financial objectives.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="50ceb-158">**谬论：迁移到云类似于切换电源开关。**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-158">**Myth: Moving to the cloud is like flipping a switch.**</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-159">迁移是一种人工密集型的技术转换。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-159">Migrations are a manually intense technical transformation.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="50ceb-160">在制定业务理由（尤其是时间敏感的理由）时，请考虑以下几个方面，这些问题可能会增加迁移资产所需的时间：</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="50ceb-160">When developing a business justification, especially justifications that are time sensitive, consider the following aspects that could increase the time it takes to migrate assets:</span></span>
